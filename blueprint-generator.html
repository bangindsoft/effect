How to Create a Blueprint Effect

The blueprint effect is a classic graphic design style widely used in technical illustrations, architectural drafts, and concept art. It transforms photography into high-contrast white vector-style lines set against a deep blue background.

Privacy Guarantee: All image processing occurs 100% locally within your browser using HTML5 Canvas API. Your files are never uploaded to external servers.

Method 1: Using the Web Generator

  1. Upload Image: Click Select Photo to choose a photo. High-contrast subjects yield the best outlines.
  2. Adjust Detail: Move the Line Detail slider to control line sensitivity.
  3. Choose Style: Toggle grid lines or change the background blue tint.
  4. Export: Click Save Result (PNG) to download your image.

Method 2: Creating Blueprint Effect in Photoshop

  1. Desaturate: Open your image and press Ctrl+Shift+U (Cmd+Shift+U on Mac).
  2. Extract Edges: Go to Filter > Stylize > Find Edges.
  3. Adjust Contrast: Open Levels (Ctrl+L) and push black/white sliders inward to clean up background noise.
  4. Invert & Colorize: Invert colors with Ctrl+I. Add a Solid Color adjustment layer with hex #002B49 and set its blend mode to Screen.
